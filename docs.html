Setting up Combo Guard

Everything you can configure, from the first minute to a fully tuned group. Most options live in one menu; the rest are short commands listed below.

Getting started

  1. Add the bot. Open @Combo_Guard_bot and pick your group, or add it from your group's member list.
  2. Make it an admin. The bot needs permission to ban users, delete messages, and create invite links. Without admin rights it can see problems but not act on them.
  3. Open the menu. Send /menu in the group, or DM the bot and pick the group from My Connected Groups.
Protection defaults are on immediately. Scam phrases, phishing domains, impersonation checks, restricted name tags, and image scanning all run from the moment the bot becomes admin. Everything below is tuning, not required setup.

The control panel

Send /menu in a group (or in DM) to open the per-group control panel. From there you can toggle and configure:

  • Anti-spam toggles for each protection type: scam phrases, contract addresses, external links and mentions, forwarded messages, invite-link phrases.
  • Custom restricted words on top of the built-in list.
  • Allowlists: your own token's contract address, trusted forward sources, and invite phrases your group actually uses.
  • Restricted name tags: extend the default list (admin, support, dev, and similar) with your own.
  • Escalation ladder: how warnings turn into mutes, kicks, and bans.
  • New-member media restriction: stop fresh joins from posting photos and videos until they have earned it.
  • Quiet hours: a daily time window where the bot enables Telegram's slow mode automatically and lifts it afterwards.
  • Welcome message, portal, captcha, logging, and daily summary settings, covered in their own sections below.

Protections

These run on every message: text, captions, the text inside images, and video keyframes. Display names are re-checked on every message too, so renaming mid-session does not work.

What gets caught

  • Scam phrases: a curated library of crypto-scam lines ("holders dm me", seed-phrase bait, signal sellers) plus pattern templates. Unicode disguises and spaced-out letters are normalized before matching.
  • Phishing domains: a built-in blocklist enforced in every group, extendable per group with your own restricted words.
  • Contract addresses: ETH and Solana addresses in messages, names, or images. Whitelist your own project's address in /menu; everything else escalates.
  • Wallet screenshots: images are read with OCR and scored against a wallet-UI model. Known spam images and videos are fingerprinted, so a repost means an instant ban.
  • Impersonators: names compared against your real admin list and the group title, with lookalike characters normalized and a fuzzy-match tolerance.
  • Restricted name tags: joins and renames claiming to be admin, support, dev, and similar roles.

Warnings and escalation

Lighter offenses earn a warning first. Every warning comes with a 10-second Undo button for the acting admin, and /remove_warn (reply to the user, or pass a user id) clears all active warnings at once. Repeat offenses climb the ladder you configured: warn, mute, kick, ban.

The global banlist

High-confidence detections (spam media, impersonation, restricted name tags) are banned in every group the bot guards, not just where they were caught. Only the bot's own detections feed this list. /gban lets a group admin push a confirmed bad actor to it; /ban stays local to your group.

Portal and captcha

The portal screens people before they enter, instead of cleaning up after them.

  1. In your group's Telegram settings, enable Approve New Members.
  2. Create a channel to act as the portal, add the bot to it, and send /id there to get the channel id.
  3. Back in the group, send /setportal_channel <channel_id>. Use /unsetportal_channel to turn it off.

Every join request is then checked against the global banlist, restricted name tags, contract addresses in the name, and impersonation of your admins or group name. Clean requests are approved; flagged ones are declined with a short audit note in the portal channel.

Captcha options

  • Math challenge: a small arithmetic prompt the joiner answers in the portal channel.
  • Image captcha: the joiner types the text shown in an image.
  • Off: instant approval once the screening passes.

The captcha type and the timeout (default 5 minutes, then the request expires) are set per group in /menu.

Welcome messages

Greet new members with text, a photo, or a video, configured in /menu. Welcome text supports placeholders:

  • {first_name}, {full_name}, {username}, {group_name}

You can attach your own buttons to the welcome message: links, or menu buttons that open in place, with control over which buttons share a row. Media is cached after the first send, so big welcome videos do not slow the bot down.

Hashtag menus

Reusable button menus any member can call up by typing #name in chat. Building one is admin-only:

  • /savemenu <name> as a reply to a message: that message becomes the menu intro.
  • /addbutton <menu> <label>|<content> adds a button. Append |same to keep it on the same row as the previous button.
  • /menus lists menus, /delbutton and /delmenu remove things.

Typical uses: #rules, #links, #faq, #buy.

Filters

Custom auto-replies. When a message matches a trigger, the bot answers with your configured response: text, image, video, or sticker, with optional buttons.

  • /filter <trigger> <response> creates one. Triggers can be plain words or patterns.
  • /filters lists them, /stop <trigger> deletes one, /edit_filter <trigger> <new> updates one.
  • /testfilter <trigger> in DM previews the response without posting it in the group.

Timed messages

Recurring announcements posted on an interval, useful for rules reminders or pinned-info refreshers.

  • /timed_add <minutes> <title>|<text> schedules one.
  • /timed_list shows them with their index numbers.
  • /timed_remove <idx> deletes, /timed_toggle <idx> pauses and resumes.

Logging channel

Route a full audit trail into a private channel only your admin team can see.

  1. Create a private channel and add the bot.
  2. Send /id in the channel to get its id.
  3. In the group, send /setlog <channel_id>. Check with /logchannel, disconnect with /unsetlog.

Each category can be switched on and off with /log <category> and /nolog <category>:

CategoryWhat it records
settingsBlocklist, welcome, rules, and filter changes
adminBans, mutes, kicks, warnings, manual actions
userJoins, leaves, self-kicks
automatedEvery automatic detection: scam text, spam media, contracts, impersonation
reportsMember /report submissions
otherNotes, filters, timed-message events

Daily summary

Every evening at 20:00, active groups get a short digest of the last 24 hours: bans, mutes, warnings, cleanup, and new arrivals compared with the day before. Groups with nothing to report are skipped, so quiet days stay quiet. The summary can be disabled per group in /menu.

Referral campaigns

A built-in growth system: members share personal invite links, and the bot verifies the people they bring actually participate.

Running a campaign (admins)

  • /setupcampaign starts one and walks you through duration and rewards.
  • /setbanner and /setendbanner attach start and end images, set up in DM.
  • /setresultschat picks the channel where standings are posted; /lastresult reposts the latest.
  • /endcampaign closes it manually. /adminhelp lists all campaign admin commands.
  • Borderline cases can be reviewed with /refdetails, /refstats, and decided with /refvalidate or /refinvalidate.

Joining in (members)

  • /ref or /getref gives a personal invite link.
  • An invited user counts as valid once they post 3 messages within 24 hours of joining.
  • /myref shows personal stats, /refleader the current top 10, /alltimeref the lifetime standings.
  • /refrules shows the campaign's payout terms; /refhelp lists the commands.

Backup and upkeep

  • /backup exports the group's whole configuration (filters, welcome, buttons, logging, timed messages, restrictions) as a file. Reply to that file with /restore in any group to import it.
  • /cleandeleted sweeps members whose Telegram accounts were deleted. It can also run automatically once a day, toggled in /menu.
  • /report, available to every member as a reply to a spam image, feeds a crowdsourced review queue: repeated flags across groups reach the bot owner.
